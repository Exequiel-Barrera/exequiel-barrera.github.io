What is Problem-Solving?

Problem-solving is the process of identifying, analyzing, and finding solutions to challenges or obstacles. It involves critical thinking, creativity, and decision-making skills to overcome difficulties and achieve desired outcomes.

Techniques for Problem-Solving

There are several techniques and approaches to problem-solving, including:

  • Pseudocode
  • Trying something
  • Rubber Ducky Method
  • Reading error messages
  • Console.logging
  • Googling
  • Asking your peers for help
  • Asking coaches for help
  • Improving your process with reflection

Critical Thinking

Critical thinking is an essential skill for effective problem-solving. It involves analyzing information, evaluating evidence, and making reasoned judgments to arrive at logical conclusions.

Collaboration

Collaboration is often necessary for solving complex problems. Working with others allows for diverse perspectives, skills, and expertise to be leveraged in finding effective solutions.

Pseudocode

I have not put this into practice much at all yet, but is a way to plan your code using plain language before actually writing the code. It helps to outline the logic and structure of the program, making it easier to translate into actual code later.

Console Logging

Googling

Probably the most used technique by far, i have been able to find answers to many of my questions by searching on the internet. There are many resources available online, such as forums, tutorials, and documentation, that can help to solve problems and learn new skills.

Asking your peers for help

I have found that asking my peers for help can be very beneficial. Sometimes, a fresh perspective can help to identify the problem and find a solution more quickly. Additionally, discussing the problem with others can lead to new insights and ideas that may not have been considered otherwise.

Asking coaches for help

Probably one the first things i would do when i get stuck, is to ask for help to one of the coaches. If i want to get an answer that , it wont be just an answer, but also an explanation of why is that way.

Improving your process with reflection

I think this is a very important step that i have not been doing enough. Reflecting on the problem-solving process can help to identify what worked well and what could be improved for future problems. It can also help to reinforce learning and build confidence in problem-solving skills.
